One hub. Three ways to know your dough.

Use it like a dashboard: understand the concept, try it in the simulator, then reflect on what actually happened.

Lessons · Understand the “why”

Short, focused slides that cover budgeting, saving, credit, debt, and more — with examples that actually match teen life instead of random textbook problems.

  • 6–12 slides per lesson so it doesn’t feel endless.
  • Quick interactive checks so you know if it clicked.
  • Visual progress across units and topics.

Example lesson flow

Budgeting · “Needs vs. Wants”

  1. Sort real teen expenses into “Need” vs “Want”.
  2. See how mislabeling changes your budget.
  3. Practice building a 50/30/20 style split.
  4. End with a reflection question you’ll see in the journal.

Budget simulator · Practice with fake money

A sandbox where you can drag dollars into categories, test “what if I buy this every month?”, and watch your goals either happen or disappear.

  • Set an income — job, allowance, side gigs.
  • Add fixed costs like subscriptions and rides.
  • See how much is truly left for wants and goals.

Money journal · Notice your patterns

Not “dear diary.” Just honest, fast answers about how you feel and what you actually did with money this week.

  • 2–3 minute entries tied to your budget runs.
  • Prompts about impulses, wins, and regrets.
  • Turn vague anxiety into specific trends.

Sample prompts

  • “What purchase this week actually made your life better?”
  • “Where did your dough go that you didn’t plan?”
  • “What’s one tiny money habit you want Future You to thank you for?”
